Coffee


Americano

Ingredients

  • 14–18 grams espresso coffee beans
  • hot or almost boiling water
  • espresso machine
  • scale
  • grinder
  • tamper






How to make

  1. Measure out your beans for a double shot. Grind them really fine.
  2. Tamp the beans, then put the portafilter into its spot on the machine.
  3. Make the espresso.
  4. Heat up the water to 70-76 degrees Celsius.
  5. Pour the espresso into the hot water, mix 1 part espresso to 2 parts water.


Lattle

Ingredients

  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 ⅓ cups hot freshly brewed dark roast espresso coffee











How to make

  1. Heat milk in a saucepan set over medium-low heat.
  2. Whisk briskly with a wire whisk to create foam.
  3. Brew espresso and pour into four cups. Pour in milk, holding back the foam with a spoon. Spoon foam over the top.


Mocha

Ingredients

  • 1 cup hot brewed coffee
  • 1 tablespoon unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 2 tablespoons milk









How to make

  1. Pour hot coffee into a mug.
  2. Stir in cocoa, sugar, and milk.


Ice Espresso

Ingredients

  • Two espresso shots
  • Ice cubes
  • A splash of water or milk










How to make

  1. Start brewing your espresso shots using your coffee brewer of choice. I'm using the AeroPress to prepare the espresso shots.
  2. After brewing the espresso coffee, let it cool slightly. You can add one or two ice cubes to speed up this process.
  3. Fill a glass with a handful of ice cubes. I like to fill my glass to about halfway, so I'm sure my coffee is nice and cold.
  4. After cooling the espressos shots, add them to the ice-filled glass.


Cappuccino

Ingredients

  • 18g ground espresso (or 1 espresso pod)
  • 150ml milk
  • cocoa powder (optional)
  • the right cup, 200-250ml capacity









How to make

  1. Make around 35ml espresso using a coffee machine and pour it into the base of your cup.
  2. Steam the milk with the steamer attachment so that it has around 4-6cm of foam on top.
  3. Hold the jug so that the spout is about 3-4cm above the cup and pour the milk in steadily.
  4. As the volume within the cup increases, bring the jug as close to the surface of the drink as possible whilst aiming to pour into the centre.
  5. Once the milk jug is almost touching the surface of the coffee, tilt the jug to speed up the rate of pour.
  6. As you accelerate, the milk will hit the back of the cup and start naturally folding in on itself to create a pattern on the top.
  7. Dust the surface with a little cocoa powder if you like.
